Geometry

Mesh

 Viscous Sublayer (y+ < 5):

  • Flow is laminar near the wall

  • Molecular viscosity dominates

  • Simple linear velocity profile: u+ = y+

  • Easy to model accurately

Log-Law Region (y+ > 30):

  • Flow is fully turbulent

  • Turbulent viscosity dominates

  • Well-established logarithmic velocity profile: u+ = (1/Îº) ln(y+) + B

  • Wall functions work perfectly here

Buffer Layer (5 < y+ < 30):

  • Transitional region between laminar and turbulent

  • Both molecular and turbulent viscosity are important

  • No simple analytical solution exists

  • Velocity profile is complex and difficult to model

  • Wall functions assume you're in log-law region, so they give inaccurate results

The "Unsafe" Nature:

  • Wall functions use log-law assumptions that don't apply in buffer layer

  • Results become unreliable and inconsistent

  • Can lead to poor convergence and inaccurate predictions
